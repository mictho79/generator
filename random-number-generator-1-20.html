The d20 is a 20-sided die used predominantly in tabletop role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons (D&D). It determines the outcome of almost every action — attack rolls, skill checks, and saving throws. Rolling a 20 (a "natural 20") is a critical success; rolling a 1 is a critical failure.

A digital d20 uses a computer's pseudo-random number generator rather than physical gravity and geometry. Both aim for uniform distribution across 20 outcomes. The digital version is faster, never gets lost, and doesn't require a table — ideal for online play.
